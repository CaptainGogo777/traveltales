Discover Japan

From the snow-capped peak of Mount Fuji to the neon-lit streets of Tokyo, Japan dazzles with contrasts.
Iconic sights like Kyoto’s golden temples, Hiroshima’s Peace Memorial, and the cherry blossoms of Yoshino reveal its deep history and reverence for nature.
Known for the precision of tea ceremonies, the energy of Shibuya Crossing, and the artistry of sushi, Japan balances serenity and innovation.
With a spirit shaped by samurai heritage, Buddhist tranquility, and cutting-edge technology, Japan enchants all who explore its timeless beauty.

Top Travel Destinations

  • Mount Fuji: Japan's iconic snow-capped volcano and UNESCO World Heritage Site, perfect for hiking or scenic views.
  • Tokyo: A dazzling metropolis blending futuristic skyscrapers, historic temples, and vibrant districts like Shibuya and Shinjuku.
  • Kyoto: The cultural heart of Japan, home to golden Kinkaku-ji, Fushimi Inari’s red torii gates, and traditional tea houses.
  • Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park : A poignant tribute to WWII history, with the iconic Atomic Bomb Dome.
  • Osaka: A food lover’s paradise with bustling Dotonbori, Osaka Castle, and world-famous street food like takoyaki.
  • Miyajima Island: The floating torii gate of Itsukushima Shrine, best seen at high tide.
  • Hakone: Hot spring retreat with views of Mount Fuji, pirate ships on Lake Ashi, and open-air museums.

Travel Tips

  • Seasonal Planning: Visit March-May for cherry blossoms or October-November for autumn foliage. Avoid Golden Week (late April-early May) for fewer crowds. Skip typhoon season (September).
  • Pack Smart: Light layers for humid summers, warm clothes for winter, slip-off shoes for temples, and a small umbrella for sudden rain.
  • Taste Safety: Enjoy sushi, ramen, and tempura at reputable spots. Try izakayas (pub-style eateries) but be cautious with raw seafood in remote areas.
  • Respect Culture: Bow when greeting, remove shoes indoors, avoid loud phone calls on trains, and never stick chopsticks upright in rice (funeral symbol).
  • Stay Protected: Use sunscreen in summer, carry hand sanitizer (few public soap dispensers), and drink tap water (safe nationwide).
  • Safe Transport: Use IC cards (Suica/Pasmo) for trains/buses, reserve Shinkansen (bullet train) seats early, and avoid rush hour (7:30-9:30am).
  • Learn Japanese Basics: “Konnichiwa” (Hello), “Arigato” (Thank you), “Sumimasen” (Excuse me), and “Ikura desu ka?” (How much?).
  • Book Early: Secure hotels for cherry blossom season, New Year’s, and Gion Matsuri (July). Book Ghibli Museum/Jiufen day trips weeks ahead.
  • Guard Against Pickpockets: Rare but possible in crowded areas like Shibuya or Shinjuku. Keep wallets in front pockets.
  • Stay Charged: Portable Wi-Fi routers > SIM cards for group travel. Outlets: 100V, Type A/B (no adapter needed for US plugs).
  • Emergency Contacts: Police (110), Ambulance (119), Tourist Hotline (050-3816-2787, English support).
  • Health Prep: No mandatory vaccines. Bring allergy meds (cedar pollen in spring) and motion sickness pills for windy mountain roads.
