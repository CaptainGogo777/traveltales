Discover Spain

From sun-drenched beaches to rugged mountain peaks, Spain’s landscapes dazzle with dramatic beauty.
Iconic landmarks like Gaudí’s Sagrada Família and the Alhambra’s Moorish splendor reveal its layered history.
The soul of Spain pulses in flamenco’s fiery rhythms, bustling tapas bars, and vibrant fiestas like La Tomatina.
Savor jamón ibérico, paella, and crisp Rioja wines—a feast of flavors shaped by diverse regions.
Born from Celtic, Roman, and Arab roots, Spain enchants with its warm spirit, where ancient traditions meet modern energy in every sunlit plaza.

Top Travel Destinations

  • Sagrada Família (Barcelona): Gaudí's unfinished masterpiece, a surreal blend of Gothic and Art Nouveau architecture.
  • Alhambra (Granada): A Moorish palace complex with intricate carvings, fountains, and panoramic views of the Sierra Nevada.
  • Park Güell (Barcelona): A whimsical public park designed by Gaudí, featuring colorful mosaics and dreamlike structures.
  • La Rambla (Barcelona): A vibrant tree-lined boulevard buzzing with street performers, markets, and outdoor cafés.
  • Prado Museum (Madrid): One of the world's finest art museums, housing masterpieces by Velázquez, Goya, and El Greco.
  • Mezquita-Catedral (Córdoba): A stunning mosque-cathedral with a forest of striped arches and a history of religious coexistence.
  • Ibiza: Famous for its electrifying nightlife, but also home to serene coves and UNESCO-listed old town.
  • Camino de Santiago: A historic pilgrimage route ending at Santiago de Compostela's majestic cathedral.
  • San Sebastián: A culinary capital with Michelin-starred pintxos bars and crescent-shaped La Concha beach.
  • Toledo: A medieval walled city where Christian, Jewish, and Moorish heritage intertwine.
  • Canary Islands: Volcanic landscapes, black-sand beaches, and year-round sunshine in Spain's Atlantic paradise.

Travel Tips

  • Seasonal Planning: Visit April-June or September-October for pleasant weather. Avoid August crowds/heat in cities. Ski season runs December-March in Pyrenees/Sierra Nevada.
  • Pack Smart: Light layers for summer, warmer clothes for winter/evenings, comfortable walking shoes for cobblestone streets, and a swimsuit for beach destinations.
  • Taste Safety: Enjoy tapas, paella, and jamón ibérico at busy restaurants. Be cautious with shellfish in summer. Tap water is safe to drink in most cities.
  • Respect Culture: Dress modestly in churches (cover shoulders/knees), observe siesta quiet hours (2-5pm in small towns), and learn basic greetings.
  • Stay Protected: Use high SPF sunscreen (even in spring), watch for pickpockets in tourist areas, and carry a reusable water bottle.
  • Safe Transport: Use Renfe for trains, ALSA for buses, and official taxis (with meters). Book high-speed AVE trains in advance.
  • Learn Spanish Basics: "Hola" (Hello), "Gracias" (Thank you), "¿Cuánto cuesta?" (How much?), "La cuenta, por favor" (Check please).
  • Book Early: Reserve accommodations for Semana Santa (Easter), August beach towns, and popular sites like Alhambra (months ahead).
  • Guard Against Theft: Use crossbody bags in crowded areas (Las Ramblas, metro), avoid displaying expensive cameras/phones.
  • Stay Charged: Bring a Type F plug adapter (220V), portable charger for day trips. Free WiFi is common in cafés.
  • Emergency Contacts: Police (112), Ambulance (061), Tourist Police (+34 902 102 112).
  • Health Prep: No required vaccines. Carry motion sickness pills for mountain roads and any personal medications.
