Discover Mexico

From Mayan ruins to colonial cities, Mexico blends ancient wonders with vibrant culture.
Taste fiery salsas, hear mariachi music, and explore landscapes from beaches to jungles.
A country where tradition and modernity dance together.

Top Travel Destinations

  • Chichén Itzá: A stunning ancient Mayan city and UNESCO World Heritage Site, home to the famous El Castillo pyramid.
  • Cancún & the Riviera Maya: Pristine beaches, turquoise waters, and world-class resorts along the Caribbean coast.
  • Mexico City: A vibrant capital with historic landmarks like the Zócalo, Frida Kahlo Museum, and Chapultepec Park.
  • Tulum: A breathtaking coastal Mayan ruin overlooking the Caribbean Sea, blending history and natural beauty.
  • Teotihuacán: The "City of the Gods," featuring the massive Pyramid of the Sun and Moon near Mexico City.
  • Oaxaca: A cultural gem known for its colonial architecture, vibrant markets, and rich indigenous traditions.
  • Copper Canyon (Barrancas del Cobre): A dramatic canyon system larger and deeper than the Grand Canyon, perfect for hiking and train rides.
  • Day of the Dead (Día de los Muertos): A UNESCO-recognized festival celebrating ancestors with colorful altars, parades, and traditions.

Travel Tips

  • Seasonal Planning: Visit between November-April (dry season) for ideal beach weather. Avoid hurricane season (June-October) in coastal areas.
  • Pack Smart: Lightweight clothing for heat, a light jacket for cooler highlands (e.g., Mexico City), and comfortable shoes for cobblestone streets.
  • Eat Safely: Enjoy tacos al pastor, mole, and fresh seafood, but choose busy food stalls with high turnover for hygiene.
  • Respect Culture: Dress modestly at religious sites (e.g., churches), ask permission before photographing indigenous communities, and greet locals with “Buenos días/tardes.”
  • Stay Protected: Use reef-safe sunscreen, insect repellent (for jungles/Chiapas), and drink bottled or purified water (avoid ice in questionable places).
  • Safe Transport: Use Uber (available in major cities) or authorized taxis (sitios). Avoid unmarked cabs, especially at night.
  • Learn Spanish Basics: “Hola” (Hello), “Gracias” (Thank you), and “¿Cuánto cuesta?” (How much?).
  • Book Early: Reserve accommodations for peak seasons like Day of the Dead (late Oct/early Nov) or Christmas.
  • Guard Against Pickpockets: Keep valuables secure in crowded areas (markets, metro) and avoid flashing expensive items.
  • Stay Charged: Bring a power bank (outlets: 127V, Type A/B). Some areas may have voltage fluctuations.
  • Emergency Contacts: Police (911), Tourist Assistance Hotline (800-006-8839), and your embassy’s local number.
  • Health Prep: Check CDC recommendations for vaccines (e.g., Hepatitis A). Carry altitude sickness meds for high-elevation cities.
